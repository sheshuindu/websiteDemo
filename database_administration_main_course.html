Description

DBAs can be divided into two categories viz., Production and Development. Production DBAs role arise only after the applications have been developed. Some of the essential activities done by the Production DBAs comprise: To ascertain the database server is running smoothly without any problems, performance maintenance tasks including backing up the database server and executing recovery operations whenever needed, and perform capacity planning considering the size and growth of data. Generally, a network administrator will become a production DBA. Development DBAs’ role is at the time of database development phase. These DBAs work along with programmers and architects in a close manner to develop a database according to the enterprise standards. Their primary activities comprise database profiling, managing user security, indexing, database creations and performance tuning. Programmers who have development experience become development DBA, as per the team’s requirements.

Benefits

  • The rising popularity of DevOps and virtualization, cloud data storage, and microservices tools imply that the role of a database administrator is constantly changing. DBAs are responsible for the integrity, performance, and security of their company’s database.
  • Their duties comprise creating, tuning, and updating new systems. But a lot of that activity has been made obsolete with the introduction of automation. However, this doesn’t mean that there is no scope for DBAs.
  • As automation progresses, DBA responsibilities will also change.
  • Progress in this occupation will be initiated by the rising data requirements of organizations in all domains of the economy.
  • Database administrators will be required to streamline and present data such that it makes it simpler for analysts and other interested people to comprehend.
  • The increasing embracing of cloud services by small and medium-sized businesses that do not consist of their own dedicated IT department could raise the employment of DBAs in this industry.
  • DBAs require the ability of professional adaptation to keep in pace with an adequately current industry and technology comprehension so as to be abreast with the latest tools. With the evolution of DevOps, more people within a company will have more understanding of the database and what makes it popular.
